Model Context Protocol & Claude

MCP (Model Context Protocol) lets Claude work directly inside your Notion workspace instead of just talking about it. By connecting the two, Claude can create pages, update databases, and search your content natively, meaning you can ask Claude to build project templates, organize notes, or manage tasks right in Notion without copying and pasting between apps.

Perfect for anyone who wants their AI assistant to actually live where they work.

Here's how to set it up:

  • Create a Notion integration - Visit notion.so/integrations, click "New Integration", name it, and select permissions like "Read content" and "Update content"
  • Get your token - Copy the "Internal Integration Token" from your integration for authentication
  • Install the MCP server - Download Node.js, then grab the Notion MCP server from the official GitHub repository
  • Configure Claude Desktop - Open Claude Desktop settings, go to Developer, click "Edit Config" to open claude_desktop_config.json, and add your server configuration with the token
  • Connect and restart - Fully quit and relaunch Claude Desktop so it can detect the MCP server and establish connection

Day 1 in Evanston

Chicagoland, I'm here! I'm thrilled to start this new chapter at Kellogg and am filled with gratitude and excitement for the next 2 years. Some highlights from the move:

  • Summmertime in Chicago - Coming back to Chicago for the first time since 2019 brought back a mix of nostalgia and deep appreciation for a city that always felt like a second home. In some ways, the city feels familiar, but I've deepened my roots and grown a few more branches since I last stepped foot in ORD.
  • Trusting strangers - My uber driver, Cassandra, noticed a phone in the backseat as I entered the car from the airport and we took it upon ourselves to track the owner's flight and try to return it. The United app had sent an alert about a gate change, and I pounced on this puzzle piece to locate the flight number while Cassandra looped back around the terminal. She asked me to sit in the driver's seat while she ran inside, which took me aback but I complied - anything for the mission. We hit a dead end when the airline representative wasn't willing to take responsibility for the device, but Cassandra was determined to get the phone back to it's owner - outside of the confines of Lyft customer service. She has the kind of warmth that comes from a lifetime of doing hard things with care. A retired detective, she's seen a thing or two.
  • Turning a space into a home - Moving is always chaotic. No toilet paper, furniture, or AC but I did have a dance party in my emtpy apartment and wandered around my new neighborhood. Feeling scattered, but grounded here.
